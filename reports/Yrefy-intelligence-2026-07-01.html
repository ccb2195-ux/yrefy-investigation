Yrefy SLP4, LLC (Delaware) formed; subscription documentation dated Feb 17, 2022 available via IRA Club. Suite 130 at 6910 E Chauncey Ln. Minimum investment $50K; up to 10.25% stated return; 5-year hold; Reg D 506(c).[13][9]

Fox News ad campaign with undisclosed paid endorsers
Yrefy runs high-frequency radio and TV advertising on Fox News and conservative media. At least 17 media personalities paid to endorse the SLP4 offering using substantially similar scripts, without required disclosures that the endorsers were compensated. Larry Elder identified by name in the MA consent order.[1][7]

FTC finalizes rules requiring disclosure of paid endorsements. This becomes the legal hook for the Massachusetts investigation into Yrefy's advertising practices.[5]

MA Securities Division initiates Docket E-2024-0334 against Yrefy LLC and Yrefy SLP4, LLC targeting the undisclosed endorsement campaign.[1]

Schoneberger claims "PCAOB audit" to potential investors
Laine Schoneberger tells potential investors Yrefy is "preparing for a PCAOB audit." Minkow notes PCAOB audits apply only to public companies, not private entities like Yrefy. No audit materialized. (Unverified — see Allegations section.)[7]

SLP5, LLC formed — new fundraising tranche
Yrefy SLP5, LLC registered at Suite 210, 6910 E Chauncey Ln (separate suite from SLP4's Suite 130). Savannah Chrisley (reality TV personality) used in SLP5 Facebook promotional posts. SLP5 registered in CT and DC.[9][11]
February 3, 2025
Massachusetts Consent Order — $2.17M total exposure ★
Commonwealth of Massachusetts Securities Division issues Consent Order (Docket E-2024-0334).

Respondents: Yrefy LLC and Yrefy SLP4, LLC
Violations: Failure to disclose 17+ paid endorsers; misleading investors about fixed return rates and early withdrawal penalties; communications not fair and balanced; targeting older Massachusetts residents.
Penalties: $750,000 administrative fine · Permanent censure & cease-and-desist · Rescission offers required to all 8 MA SLP4 investors (total ~$1,418,000 invested)
Total exposure: ~$2.17M[1][3][4]

High Priority
SLP5 endorser disclosures post-MA consent order
Savannah Chrisley's SLP5 Facebook promos appear to postdate the Feb 2025 MA consent order. If those materials lack proper paid-endorser disclosures, Yrefy may be in violation of the cease-and-desist order it just signed. Compare SLP5 ad materials against the MA consent order terms and FTC endorsement disclosure requirements. Check whether AZ DIFI was notified.
High Priority
Verify or refute the PCAOB audit claim
Schoneberger told potential investors in ~Q4 2024 that Yrefy was "preparing for a PCAOB audit." PCAOB audits apply only to SEC-registered public companies, not private LLCs. If this statement was made in investor communications, it may constitute a material misrepresentation under securities law. Request Yrefy's investor communications from late 2024 via rescission offer process (MA investors have this right) or from the MA case file.
Medium Priority
SLP2 — the missing fund
SEC Form D confirms SLP1 and SLP3 exist. SLP2 is conspicuously absent from all registries searched. Either it was never formed, failed to file Form D (itself a violation), or was formed under an alternate name. Search SEC EDGAR directly for "Yrefy SLP2" and Arizona SOS records. If SLP2 raised money without a Form D, that is a separate securities law violation.
Medium Priority
Actual beneficial owners of SLP4 and SLP5
The DC Beneficial Owners registry confirmed entity registration status but did not surface individual owner names. Check FinCEN's BOI (Beneficial Ownership Information) database for CTA filings, which may have been required for the SLP entities. The IRA Club subscription docs (public) list investor certification requirements but not the actual investors.
Medium Priority
State licensing in excluded jurisdictions
Yrefy excludes CA, NY, WA, IN, CT, ME and other states from its offering. Determine whether these are voluntary exclusions or the result of denied licensing applications or regulatory bars. Contact each state's securities division directly — a denied application is a different risk signal than a voluntary choice to exclude a state.
Medium Priority
Fenstermaker's NextStudent background
NextStudent was among the largest privately held student loan companies before the 2010 private student loan market collapse. Confirm whether it wound down in good standing or under financial/regulatory pressure. Check EDGAR for any SEC filings, and Arizona/Delaware SOS for dissolution records. This would contextualize Fenstermaker's risk profile significantly.

Allegations & Unverified Claims

The following claims have not been adjudicated or independently verified. They are reported as disclosed by their sources, not as established facts.

Unverified — Source: Barry Minkow, Substack (Early 2026)[7]
Ponzi-like structure alleged. Minkow claims Yrefy pays existing investors primarily from new investor capital rather than from sustainable loan portfolio performance — a classic Ponzi hallmark. He cites the heavy radio advertising as evidence of a "desperate need to raise new money." No audited financials have been provided to investors to confirm or deny this. Credibility caveat: Minkow is a convicted fraudster (ZZZZ Best carpet-cleaning fraud, 1988) who now runs a fraud investigation newsletter. His allegations require independent verification before being treated as findings.
Unverified — Source: Barry Minkow, Substack (Early 2026)[7]
No audited financial statements. Minkow states Yrefy has never provided audited financial statements to potential investors. The company's default rate data (2017–2025) is explicitly noted on its disclosures page as "not independently audited." Schoneberger's claim of a pending PCAOB audit (which applies only to public companies) is alleged to have been used to deflect investor requests for audited financials.
Unverified — Source: Facebook / Student Loan Justice Group
Predatory borrower practices alleged. A post in the Student Loan Justice Group Facebook community accused Yrefy of predatory practices toward borrowers. No adjudicated finding; no specific named complainant identified in public sources.
Adjudicated / Confirmed — Source: MA Securities Division[1]
Note on the MA consent order: The violations found by Massachusetts — undisclosed paid endorsements and materially misleading investor communications — are confirmed findings by a regulator, not allegations. They are listed here only to distinguish them from the unverified claims above. Yrefy agreed to the consent order without admitting or denying the specific findings.
