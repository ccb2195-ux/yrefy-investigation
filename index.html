Executive Summary

⚠ This report documents a pattern of potential securities fraud indicators: unregistered offerings, mathematically unsustainable return promises, admitted state securities violations, a fund series with no SEC filing of any kind, and comparison by a professional fraud investigator to a $650 million Ponzi scheme. No court has yet adjudicated fraud. All allegations are documented and sourced below.

Yrefy LLC is an Arizona-based company that refinances defaulted private student loans. Since approximately 2018, it has raised investor capital through a series of Regulation D private placement offerings (SLP1–SLP5), promising accredited investors fixed annual returns of 6.5–10.25%. The company has no independent audit, its two principals have no current securities registrations, one entire fund series (SLP2) has no SEC filing of any kind, and its advertised borrower interest rates (max ~5.99%) are mathematically insufficient to cover its promised investor returns while also funding tens of millions in television advertising.

Per his last Form U4, Schoneberger is listed as an Investment Adviser Representative at Newmark Capital Management LLC. Yrefy does not appear on this U4 filing.[3]

Yrefy LLC founded
Fenstermaker and Schoneberger co-found Yrefy. Neither has current securities registration. The company begins refinancing defaulted private student loans and raising investor capital — with no Form D filings yet recorded for this period.[5]
January 29, 2020
Yrefy SLP3 Form D filed — before SLP1
SLP3's first Form D is filed with the SEC under 506(b), reporting $670,000 raised. Notably, SLP3 predates SLP1's first filing by 11 months, suggesting numbering is not chronological and earlier offerings may have operated without filing.[6]
April 22, 2020
SLP3 switches from 506(b) to 506(c)
506(b) prohibits general solicitation (i.e., TV advertising). The switch to 506(c) suggests Yrefy was advertising on television for an offering that did not permit it. SLP1 never makes this switch and stays on 506(b) throughout.[6]
December 10, 2020
Yrefy SLP1 Form D filed — $2.8M already raised on first filing
SLP1's initial Form D reports $2,800,000 already raised under Rule 506(b). The amount suggests the offering had been active prior to filing, potentially without a valid Reg D exemption.[5]
April 30, 2021
SLP1 reaches $3.9M; SLP3 reaches $10M; SLP4 first filed
A cluster of amendments and new filings. SLP4 uses 506(c) from the start, consistent with active TV advertising. At no point is there any Form D filing for a "Yrefy SLP2 LLC" entity.[7]
July 12, 2022
SLP4 amended — $7.045M raised
Yrefy SLP4 LLC (CIK: 0001859283) reports $7,045,000 raised under Rule 506(c). Heavy Fox News advertising confirmed during this period by fraud investigator Barry Minkow.[2][8]
2021–2024
Fox News and CNBC advertising campaign
Yrefy airs heavy advertising on Fox News and CNBC. Charlie Kirk appears in a Fox segment featuring Yrefy. 17+ paid celebrity and media endorsers are used, none of whom are disclosed as compensated promoters to investors.[18][19]
2024
Schoneberger falsely claims "PCAOB audit" coming in Q1 2025
When asked for audited financials, Schoneberger tells a prospective investor Yrefy is "preparing for a PCAOB audit." PCAOB oversight applies only to auditors of publicly traded companies. A private LLC cannot receive a PCAOB audit. No audit ever occurred.[10][22]
February 3, 2025
Massachusetts Consent Order — $750K fine + $1.418M rescission
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts Securities Division (Docket E-2024-0334) issues a Consent Order finding violations of M.G.L. c. 110A §101. Yrefy admits: undisclosed paid endorsers (17+), false "10.25% guaranteed" return claims, false "no attack on your principal" statements, and false claim of independent collateral agent (Yrefy itself is the collateral agent per the Security Agreement). Fine: $750,000. Required rescission offers to 8 MA investors on $1,418,000.[1][11]
April 2025
Federal lawsuit filed: Mentor et al. v. Yrefy SLP5 LLC
Marvin Mentor, Melinda Emonyon, and Victor Emonyon file a Breach of Contract complaint against Yrefy SLP5 LLC in federal court, related to U.S. Department of Education loans refinanced by Yrefy. Case active as of November 2025.[17]
February 2026
Barry Minkow publishes detailed fraud analysis
Minkow — a convicted fraudster turned fraud investigator who has exposed multiple Ponzi schemes — publishes analysis comparing Yrefy's Fox News advertising pattern to the NRIA scheme ($650M Ponzi). He offers to fund a forensic audit and alerts Fox News, which declines to act.[8][9]
July 2, 2025
SLP4 new Form D filed — $0 raised yet (new fundraising round)
A new Form D is filed under the Yrefy SLP4 entity, reporting zero dollars raised so far. Suggests the company has exhausted prior investor pools and is actively seeking fresh capital.[2]

Key Findings

Critical
The Math Is Impossible Without New Money Funding Old Returns
Yrefy charges borrowers a maximum ~5.99% interest while promising investors up to 10.25% annually. On top of this, the company spent an estimated $5.4M+ on television advertising and $726,500+ on a single celebrity endorser. There is no disclosed revenue source that bridges this gap. The PPM (page 67) explicitly states that proceeds from new offerings will be used for "refinancing of private student loans, the purchase of previous SLPs, and the retirement of previous investors" — the textbook Ponzi disclosure phrase.[15]
Critical
Yrefy SLP2 Has No SEC Filing of Any Kind
Searches of SEC EDGAR, FormDs.com, and NASAA's Electronic Filing Depository return zero results for any entity named "Yrefy SLP2 LLC." SLP1, SLP3, SLP4, and SLP5 all have Form D filings. SLP2 does not. Under Securities Act Section 5, selling securities without a valid exemption is a federal violation.[23] Investors in SLP2 hold an absolute rescission right under Section 12(a)(1).[16] The SEC has no visibility into how much SLP2 raised or how many investors it affected.[7]
Critical
Admitted State Securities Violations — Massachusetts Consent Order
The February 3, 2025 Consent Order (Docket E-2024-0334) contains admitted facts: (1) 17+ paid endorsers whose compensation was never disclosed to investors; (2) false claims that returns of "up to" 10.25% were guaranteed; (3) false statements that investor principal was fully protected when the PPM itself contains no such guarantee; (4) false representation that an independent collateral agent held security — the Security Agreement names Yrefy itself as collateral agent.[1][24]
Critical
PCAOB Audit Misrepresentation
CIO Schoneberger told a prospective investor that Yrefy was "preparing for a PCAOB audit" to occur in Q1 2025. PCAOB (Public Company Accounting Oversight Board) is established under Sarbanes-Oxley and oversees auditors of SEC-registered public companies only. A private LLC cannot obtain a PCAOB audit. This statement was factually impossible and designed to create a false impression of regulatory oversight. No audit has occurred; the investor site now says "has not been independently audited."[10][21][22]
Significant
Both Principals Unregistered — No FINRA or SEC IA Registration
Donald Fenstermaker (CEO/Chairman): Zero results in FINRA BrokerCheck. Has never been a registered broker-dealer or investment adviser representative in any jurisdiction.[12]

Nathan Laine Schoneberger (CIO): CRD #2715309. Last registered May 2014 (First Financial Equity Corp). Has been unregistered for 12 years while running Yrefy's capital-raising operations. Has 3 customer dispute disclosures: churning allegation (2012, $36K), borrowing $75K from a client and not repaying it (2007, $111K claimed), and inappropriate insurance products (2006). All closed without payment but on permanent record.[3][4]
Significant
SLP1 Raised Under 506(b) — Which Prohibits TV Advertising
Yrefy SLP1 filed all Form Ds under Rule 506(b), which prohibits "general solicitation" — a category that includes television advertising. If Yrefy aired Fox News or CNBC ads while SLP1 was the active offering (2020–2021), those ads constituted general solicitation for an offering that did not permit it, potentially invalidating the 506(b) exemption for all SLP1 investors and triggering rescission rights.[5][23]
Significant
Fraud Investigator Barry Minkow Flags Yrefy as NRIA-Pattern Risk
Minkow — who exposed ZZZZ Best (1987) and has subsequently identified multiple Ponzi schemes — published detailed analysis identifying the same Fox News advertising pattern in Yrefy that preceded the NRIA collapse. NRIA raised $650M from investors, including $90M from elderly investors, before its 2022 collapse. Minkow specifically flagged the "retirement of previous investors" PPM language and the advertising intensity as desperation-fundraising indicators. He offered to pay for an independent forensic audit and alerted Fox News; Fox declined to stop airing Yrefy ads.[8][9]
Notable
SLP3 Filed Before SLP1 — Chronology Anomaly
Yrefy SLP3's first Form D was filed January 29, 2020 — 11 months before SLP1's first filing. The SLP1 filing reported $2.8M already raised on its initial Form D (filed December 2020). These facts together suggest either: (a) some SLP series were operating for extended periods before filing, or (b) the series numbers do not correspond to launch order. Either scenario implies earlier fundraising rounds not captured in any SEC filing.[5][6]
